## 4. APPROACH & METHODOLOGY

### Phase 1: Development & Pre-Production (Weeks 1–4)

**Creative Development**
- Collaborate with Elevate team to define series format, episode structure, and tone
- Develop a series bible: episode arc, visual style guide, music/sound direction, graphics package
- Research each Prize winner's story to identify the most compelling narrative angle
- Define target episode length based on YouTube analytics (recommended: 12–18 minutes for main episodes, 60–90 seconds for Shorts/social cuts)

**Production Planning**
- Build a 12-month production calendar mapped to Prize winner locations and availability
- Identify and vet local crews in key markets (U.S. cities, India, Africa)
- Establish equipment packages for different scenarios (studio interview, field/verite, travel-light international)
- Set up media asset management pipeline (ingest → edit → review → delivery)
- Establish vendor agreements and travel protocols

**Channel Strategy**
- Audit current YouTube channel performance (watch time, CTR, retention curves, subscriber sources)
- Develop a content calendar optimized for YouTube's algorithm — consistent uploads, strategic Shorts, Community posts, premieres
- Define SEO/metadata strategy: titles, descriptions, tags, thumbnails, end screens
- Benchmark against comparable channels (Bloomberg Originals, Business Insider, VICE Impact)

### Phase 2: Production (Months 2–11)

**Per-Episode Production Workflow**
1. **Pre-interview & research** — remote call with subject, background research, location scouting
2. **Production** — 2–3 day shoot per episode (verite/day-in-the-life footage + sit-down interview + b-roll)
3. **Post-production** — editing, color grade, sound mix, graphics, music licensing, subtitles/captions
4. **Social cuts** — 3–5 short-form clips per episode for Instagram Reels, TikTok, X, YouTube Shorts
5. **Review & delivery** — Elevate review cycle (2 rounds), final delivery to channel

**Estimated Output (12-month period)**
- **8–12 main episodes** (depending on complexity and travel requirements)
- **30–50 social cuts** (short-form content derived from main episodes)
- **Supplementary content** — behind-the-scenes, community posts, premiere events

**Production Quality Standards**
- 4K capture, professional audio (lav + boom), cinematic lighting
- Color-graded with consistent series look
- Closed captions and subtitles (English + local language where applicable)
- Royalty-free or licensed music
- Consistent graphics package and series branding

### Phase 3: Optimization & Growth (Ongoing)

- Monthly analytics review — audience demographics, retention, CTR
- A/B test thumbnails and titles
- Adjust format based on performance data
- Community engagement strategy
- Cross-promotion with Prize winners' channels and social media
- Quarterly strategy report to Elevate team

### International Production Approach

For episodes in India and Africa:
- Hire vetted local crews (camera, sound, fixers/translators)
- Travel to direct and produce on-site
- Travel-light equipment strategy supplemented by local rentals
- Consolidate multiple shoots per travel trip for efficiency
- Handle all logistics: travel, permits, visas, insurance, equipment customs
